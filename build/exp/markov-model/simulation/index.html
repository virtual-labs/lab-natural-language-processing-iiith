Instructions
  1. Select a corpus from the dropdown menu. By default, 'Corpus A' is selected and loaded.
  2. Observe the displayed transition and emission probabilities computed from the training data.
  3. Enter probability values for each state transition and emission in the Markov model simulation. All input fields are available at once.
  4. Click the "Check" button to validate your answers. Correct values are retained; incorrect ones can be revised. Feedback is provided for each attempt.
  5. If you need help, click "Show Hint" for guidance. To see the correct answers and compare them with your input, click "Show Answer" (only after entering your answers).
  6. Once all answers are correct, the simulation displays the final probability calculations and results.
  7. To start over or switch to a different corpus, use the "Reset" button (which always resets to Corpus A) or select a new corpus from the dropdown.
